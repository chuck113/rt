1

Nas

Illmatic (1994)

Often considered the best rap album.  So fluid, creative, and intelligent and that's just the lyrics.  Nas flows at an incredible speed spouting a large vocabulary about life on the streets.  Takes a few listens but the payoff is huge.  Highly recommended.
 
2

Wu-Tang Clan

Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers) (1993)

Just a fierce barage of excellent rap technique and production.  Often, skits in rap albums fall short but in this case, they do much to strengthen the album's mood and overall charm, two facets of that are large components of why this album is so great.  You are introduced to so many scenerios and characters that you instantly fall in love with the hard hitting style of the album.

Production:10Cemented RZA as the best producer on the East Coast.
Lyrics: 8.  Very innovative and fun.
Delivery/Flow:9Every rapper in the Tang is unique and talented and you get a lot of different styles that make the whole flow of the album that much better.

5

Eric B. & Rakim

Paid In Full (1987)

Man, where do I even start.  Let's just say that rap can be split into two different eras, at least in it's initial stages.  Before PIF and After.  With one of the best DJ's of the time in both raw talent and his later influences and arguably the best rapper of his time, Rakim.  In the way John Coltrane could touch one spirtually through his long and beutiful musical exhibitions, Rakim does here with his worldplay and metaphoric, mulitple-layred rhymes.  Just nasty and unparalled at that point.  Infleuned many in many different ways, most notably Nas.  Put the two talents together and you get Paid In Full, the best rap album of the 1980s.

Production:  9.  Eric B. is the Mo'fuckin President.
Lyrics: 10.  Rakim is a top 4 MC if not the best.
Delivery/Flow:10.  Rakim basically invented flow.

9

N.W.A

Straight Outta Compton (1988)

Influenced rap in both its emergence as a storytelling genre as well as a more raunchy genre.  Had brilliant personality, much like polar opposite of the time, Public Enemy that captivated the country, from the streets of Watts to the middle America.  Easy's flair, Ice Cube's mic skill/writing, Ren's raps, and Dre's Production were in top form here and the album would eventually catipult them all to different degrees of success.  Just a fun album, with some of rap's most memorable and influencial tracks.  Made being real okay.

93

Jay-Z

The Black Album (2003)

When you take the man with the best rap resume ever and put him together with the best producers in the country, guys like Just Blayze, Rick Rubin, Kanye West, and Pharrell, you are going to get a classic and an album I feel is very underrated.  Many pass it off as just good mainly because it sounds very pop but I feel that's a terrible way to look at the album.  It's a career retrospective that gets pretty personal, much more than other rap albums, and is very honest.  The claims he makes here, he backs them up.  He basically asks everyone to look what he's done, compare it to the rest, and see that noone has beaten his combination of consistancy, chart dominance, flow, choice of production, and overall presence as a rapper.

Production:  9.  All the big names are here.
Lyrics:  8.  Very well written album making many boasts.
Delivery/Flow:  8.  Arguablly the best flow of any rapper ever and if not that, then right up there with the Biggies and Slick Ricks..
